## Reading Article

### The Surprising Strength of Slower Decisions

In modern life, speed often looks like intelligence. People who answer quickly seem confident. Teams that decide in ten minutes look efficient. Online, the fastest reply often gets the most attention. Because of this, many people start to believe that good decision-making means immediate decision-making. But real life keeps teaching the opposite lesson. Some of our most expensive mistakes happen not because we are foolish, but because we are too eager to finish thinking. Speed is useful in emergencies, but in ordinary life, speed can easily become a habit that hides weak judgment.

在現代生活中，速度常常看起來像是智慧。回答很快的人似乎很有自信。十分鐘內做出決定的團隊看起來很有效率。在網路上，最快的回覆也常常得到最多注意力。因此，許多人開始相信，好的決策就是立刻做決策。然而真實生活一再教我們相反的道理。我們有些代價高昂的錯誤，並不是因為我們愚蠢，而是因為我們太急著結束思考。速度在緊急情況下很有用，但在日常生活裡，速度很容易變成一種掩蓋判斷薄弱的習慣。

Consider how often this happens in small daily moments. A shopper sees a discount and buys something unnecessary before comparing prices. A student chooses the easiest presentation topic and later discovers that there is not enough material. A worker says yes to a new task without checking the deadline, then feels trapped for the rest of the week. None of these situations sounds dramatic, yet they all follow the same pattern: a quick choice creates a longer problem. The person saves a few seconds at the beginning and loses hours later trying to repair the result.

想想看，這種事在日常小時刻裡有多常發生。購物者看到折扣，還沒比價就買了其實不需要的東西。學生選了最容易的報告題目，後來才發現資料根本不夠。上班族沒有確認截止日期就答應新任務，接著整週都覺得被困住。這些情況聽起來都不算戲劇化，但它們遵循的是同一種模式：快速的選擇，造成更長久的問題。這個人在一開始省下幾秒鐘，後來卻花了好幾個小時修補後果。

One reason we rush is emotional discomfort. Slowing down forces us to stay with uncertainty, and uncertainty is unpleasant. When we pause, we must admit that we do not fully understand the situation yet. We may worry that other people will think we are weak, unprepared, or indecisive. In many workplaces and schools, confidence is rewarded more visibly than careful thought. Saying "I need more time to think" does not sound as impressive as answering immediately. However, the quiet person who asks for an hour may avoid the embarrassing mistake that the confident person makes in five seconds.

我們之所以匆忙，其中一個原因是情緒上的不舒服。放慢腳步會逼我們和不確定感待在一起，而不確定感令人不愉快。當我們暫停時，我們必須承認自己還沒有完全理解情況。我們也可能擔心別人會覺得我們很弱、準備不足，或優柔寡斷。在許多職場和學校裡，自信往往比仔細思考更容易被看見與獎勵。說「我需要多一點時間想想」聽起來，沒有立刻作答那麼厲害。然而，那個安靜地要求一小時的人，可能正好避開了那位五秒內自信作答的人會犯下的尷尬錯誤。

Technology also trains us to move too fast. Many apps are carefully designed to remove friction. With one tap, we can order food, send money, post an opinion, cancel a plan, or react to a message. Convenience is not the enemy; it often improves life. The problem is that it removes the natural pauses that once protected us from ourselves. In the past, writing an angry letter took time. Now an angry message can cross the world before the sender has even taken one deep breath. Faster tools do not automatically create wiser behavior.

科技也在訓練我們動得太快。許多 app 都被精心設計成盡量減少阻力。只要點一下，我們就能訂餐、轉帳、發表意見、取消計畫，或回覆訊息。便利本身不是敵人；它常常讓生活更好。問題在於，它拿掉了那些原本能保護我們免於衝動行事的自然停頓。以前寫一封憤怒的信需要時間。現在，一則憤怒訊息可能在寄件人連一次深呼吸都還沒做之前，就已經傳到世界另一端。更快的工具，並不會自動創造更有智慧的行為。

This is why slower decisions are not the same as weak decisions. To slow down does not mean to hesitate forever. It means creating a small space between impulse and action. That space may be only a minute long. You might walk to get water, write two possible choices on paper, or ask a simple question such as, "What problem am I actually trying to solve?" Very often, the quality of a decision improves the moment the real question becomes clear. People are not always wrong because they choose badly. Sometimes they are wrong because they solve the wrong problem.

這就是為什麼較慢的決定，不等於軟弱的決定。放慢並不表示永遠猶豫不決。它的意思是在衝動與行動之間，創造一個小小的空間。那個空間可能只有一分鐘。你也許只是去倒杯水、把兩個可能選擇寫在紙上，或問一個簡單問題，例如：「我現在真正想解決的是什麼問題？」很多時候，只要真正的問題變清楚了，決策品質就會立刻提升。人們不一定是因為選得差而出錯；有時候，他們是因為一開始就在解決錯的問題。

Money decisions show this clearly. People often imagine that financial problems come only from low salaries or bad luck. In reality, many money problems grow from repeated emotional choices. A tired person buys things for comfort. A stressed person pays for convenience without calculating the long-term cost. Someone who feels insecure purchases expensive items to appear successful. Each choice may feel small, but repeated small choices become a heavy monthly burden. Slower thinking does not remove emotion, yet it prevents temporary emotion from writing long-term financial plans.

金錢決策最能清楚說明這一點。人們常以為財務問題只來自低薪或運氣不好。實際上，很多金錢問題是由反覆的情緒性選擇慢慢長出來的。疲累的人會為了安慰自己而購物。壓力大的人會為了方便付錢，卻沒有計算長期成本。缺乏安全感的人會購買昂貴物品，好讓自己看起來成功。每個選擇當下都可能感覺很小，但一連串小選擇會變成沉重的每月負擔。較慢的思考不會消除情緒，卻能防止短暫情緒替你寫下長期的財務計畫。

Relationships are another area where speed can be dangerous. A fast reply during an argument may feel powerful, but it often creates damage that takes much longer to repair. Many people do not really want to hurt others; they simply want to end their own discomfort quickly. They send the message, make the accusation, or use the sharp joke because it gives immediate emotional release. Unfortunately, relief for one minute can create distance for six months. In human relationships, accuracy, kindness, and timing usually matter more than verbal speed.

人際關係則是另一個速度可能很危險的領域。爭執中的快速回覆，也許當下讓人覺得有力量，但它常常造成需要更久時間修復的傷害。許多人其實不是真的想傷害別人；他們只是想趕快結束自己的不舒服。因此他們傳出那則訊息、做出那句指責，或說出那個尖銳的玩笑，因為那能帶來立即的情緒釋放。不幸的是，一分鐘的舒緩，可能造成六個月的距離。在人際關係中，精準、善意與時機，通常比口頭反應速度更重要。

Education also rewards the wrong kind of speed sometimes. Students may think the best learners are the ones who answer first and speak most smoothly. But serious learning is often slower than performance. A strong learner notices confusion, checks meaning, and asks follow-up questions. That process may look less impressive in the moment, especially in a classroom that values quick participation. Yet over time, the student who pauses to understand usually builds stronger skills than the student who rushes to appear smart. Real progress is often quiet before it becomes visible.

教育有時候也會獎勵錯的那種速度。學生可能以為，最會學的人就是最先回答、講得最流暢的人。但真正扎實的學習，往往比表現看起來更慢。強的學習者會注意到自己的困惑、確認意思，並追問後續問題。這個過程在當下看起來可能沒那麼亮眼，尤其是在重視快速參與的課堂裡。然而，隨著時間過去，那個願意停下來理解的學生，通常比急著表現聰明的學生建立出更扎實的能力。真正的進步，常常在變得看得見之前，是安靜的。

Of course, not every choice deserves deep analysis. Spending twenty minutes choosing a sandwich is not wisdom. The goal is not to slow down everything. The goal is to identify which decisions carry consequences beyond the present moment. A useful test is simple: "Will this still matter tomorrow, next week, or next month?" If the answer is yes, then some extra thinking is usually a smart investment. If the answer is no, moving quickly may be perfectly fine. Good judgment is not about always being slow. It is about knowing when speed is too expensive.

當然，不是每個選擇都值得深入分析。花二十分鐘選三明治，並不是智慧。目標不是把所有事都放慢。目標是辨認出哪些決定會帶來超過當下的後果。有一個很實用的測試很簡單：「這件事到了明天、下週或下個月，還重要嗎？」如果答案是重要，那麼多花一點時間思考通常就是聰明的投資。如果答案是不重要，那快速行動完全可能沒有問題。好的判斷力不是永遠都慢，而是知道什麼時候速度太昂貴。

Many calm decision-makers are not naturally calm people. They simply build systems that protect them from unnecessary urgency. They sleep before replying to emotional emails. They write drafts but delay sending them. They compare options in writing instead of trusting memory alone. They ask one more question before spending money. These habits look ordinary, but they create extraordinary results over time. Better decisions rarely come from magic. More often, they come from humble routines that give truth enough time to catch up with emotion.

很多冷靜的決策者，其實天生並不冷靜。他們只是建立起一套能保護自己、避免不必要緊迫感的系統。他們在回覆情緒化 email 之前先睡一覺。他們先寫草稿，但延後送出。他們把選項寫下來比較，而不是只靠記憶。他們在花錢之前再多問一個問題。這些習慣看起來很普通，卻會隨著時間累積出不普通的結果。更好的決定很少來自魔法。更多時候，它來自那些謙遜的小習慣，讓真相有足夠時間追上情緒。

For language learners, this lesson matters in a special way. Many learners want instant fluency, instant confidence, and instant perfection. Because of that, they may avoid difficult sentences, skip careful review, or guess meanings too quickly. But language grows through repeated, thoughtful contact. If you slow down long enough to notice why a sentence works, why a collocation sounds natural, or why your first guess was wrong, your progress becomes more durable. Sometimes the fastest road to improvement begins with the courage to pause and look more carefully.

對語言學習者來說，這個道理特別重要。很多學習者想要立刻流利、立刻有自信、立刻完美。因此，他們可能會避開困難句子、跳過仔細複習，或太快猜意思。然而，語言能力是靠反覆而有意識的接觸慢慢長出來的。如果你願意放慢，去注意一個句子為什麼成立、一個搭配為什麼自然，或你第一次猜測為什麼錯了，你的進步就會更穩固。有時候，最快的進步之路，正是從有勇氣停下來、看得更仔細開始。

## Grammar Focus

### 1. `not because ... but because ...`

- Use this pattern to correct a false explanation and give the real reason.
- Example: *Some mistakes happen not because we are foolish, but because we are too eager to finish thinking.*
- Your turn: *He failed not because he lacked talent, but because he did not prepare carefully.*

### 2. `The goal is not to A. The goal is to B.`

- Use this pattern to contrast a wrong focus with the right one.
- Example: *The goal is not to slow down everything. The goal is to identify important decisions.*
- Your turn: *The goal is not to memorize every word. The goal is to understand how words are used.*

### 3. `If ..., then ...`

- Use this to show a condition and its logical result.
- Example: *If the answer is yes, then some extra thinking is usually a smart investment.*
- Your turn: *If I feel angry, then I should wait before replying.*

## Confusing Words

### 1. quick / fast / immediate

- **quick**: 強調動作花的時間短  
  Example: *She gave a quick answer.*
- **fast**: 強調速度快  
  Example: *This is a fast train.*
- **immediate**: 強調毫不延遲、立刻發生  
  Example: *He wanted an immediate reply.*

### 2. choose / decide

- **choose**: 從選項中挑選  
  Example: *I chose the cheaper plan.*
- **decide**: 做出決定，常接不定詞或子句  
  Example: *I decided to wait one more day.*

### 3. quiet / calm

- **quiet**: 安靜的，聲音少  
  Example: *The room was quiet in the morning.*
- **calm**: 冷靜的，情緒穩定的  
  Example: *She stayed calm during the discussion.*

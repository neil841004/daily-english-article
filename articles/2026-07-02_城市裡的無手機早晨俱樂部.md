## 複習區

### 舊單字複習
1. **practical**：實際的，務實的。  
   Quick check: *We need a practical plan, not just a beautiful idea.*
2. **unavoidable**：無法避免的。  
   Quick check: *Some changes feel unavoidable, but we can still respond wisely.*
3. **stability**：穩定。  
   Quick check: *Financial stability often grows slowly, not suddenly.*
4. **cherished**：被珍惜的。  
   Quick check: *The old photo became a cherished memory for the family.*
5. **relevant**：仍然相關的，有現實意義的。  
   Quick check: *A tradition survives when it stays relevant to modern life.*

### 舊文法複習
1. **had been open since + 時間點**：表示某狀態從過去某時間持續到另一個過去時間。  
   Example: *The theater had been open since the 1960s.*
2. **not only ... but also ...**：用來連接兩個並列且同樣重要的內容。  
   Example: *The project not only saved the building but also rebuilt trust.*

### 舊搭配詞複習
1. **turn off the lights**：熄燈，也可延伸為結束營業。  
   Prompt: What kind of place in your city might turn off the lights forever?
2. **spread quickly**：快速傳開。  
   Prompt: What kind of news spreads quickly in your friend group?
3. **gain attention**：得到關注。  
   Prompt: How can a small local event gain attention online?

## 今日文章

### The Club That Meets Before the City Wakes

At 6:10 every morning, when the streets are still quiet and the convenience stores are only beginning to fill with light, a small group gathers in a public library in Taipei. They are not there for a class, a meeting, or a formal event. They come for something much simpler and, in modern life, much rarer: ninety minutes without their phones. On a long wooden table near the windows, they place their devices in a cloth box, pour tea into paper cups, open notebooks, and begin the day in silence. For office workers, university students, freelancers, and one retired engineer, this strange routine has become more valuable than sleeping a little longer.

每天早上 6 點 10 分，當街道仍然安靜、便利商店才剛亮起燈光時，一小群人會聚集在台北的一間公共圖書館裡。他們不是去上課、開會，也不是參加正式活動。他們是為了一件更簡單、也在現代生活中更稀有的事而來：九十分鐘不用手機。在靠窗的長木桌上，他們把裝置放進布盒裡，將茶倒進紙杯，打開筆記本，然後安靜地開始一天。對上班族、大學生、自由工作者，以及一位退休工程師來說，這個奇怪的習慣已經比多睡一點更有價值。

The club began almost by accident. Last winter, a graduate student named Yuki noticed that her mornings were disappearing into short videos, unread messages, and the anxious habit of checking news she could not control. She told a friend that she missed the feeling of starting the day with her own mind instead of someone else’s feed. The friend laughed and said, “Then borrow your morning back.” They invited two more people to meet before work, and the rule was simple: no phone use unless there was a family emergency. Nobody expected the idea to last more than a week. Instead, people kept returning, partly because the rule was clear and partly because they felt calmer after only a few sessions.

這個俱樂部幾乎是意外開始的。去年冬天，一位名叫 Yuki 的研究生注意到，她的早晨正消失在短影片、未讀訊息，以及那種反覆查看自己無法控制之新聞的焦慮習慣裡。她告訴朋友，自己很想念那種用自己的大腦開始一天，而不是用別人的動態開始一天的感覺。朋友笑著說：「那就把早晨借回來吧。」她們又邀了另外兩個人在上班前碰面，規則很簡單：除非有家庭緊急狀況，否則不能用手機。沒有人預期這個點子會持續超過一週。結果，人們一直回來，一部分是因為規則很清楚，另一部分是因為他們只參加幾次就感到更平靜。

What happens in those ninety minutes is not dramatic. One member reads an English article and writes down five useful expressions. Another plans the day on paper because paper, he says, does not beg for attention every eight seconds. A software designer sketches interface ideas. The retired engineer copies a poem by hand to improve his memory and handwriting. Sometimes the group speaks for ten minutes at the end, but often they leave with only a nod. This may sound too quiet to matter, yet that quietness is exactly the point. The club does not try to entertain people. It protects a kind of attention that many people feel they have lost but do not know how to rebuild.

那九十分鐘裡發生的事其實並不戲劇化。有位成員會讀一篇英文文章，寫下五個實用片語。另一位會把一天的安排寫在紙上，因為他說紙張不會每八秒就乞求你的注意力。某位軟體設計師會畫介面草圖。那位退休工程師則會手抄詩句，來鍛鍊記憶與字跡。有時候，大家會在最後聊十分鐘；但很多時候，他們只是點個頭就離開。這聽起來也許安靜得不值得一提，但那份安靜正是重點。這個俱樂部不是要娛樂人，它是在保護一種許多人覺得自己已經失去、卻不知道該怎麼重建的專注力。

Psychologists often explain attention as a limited resource. If that is true, then many digital tools are built like shops on the same narrow street, each trying to pull your sleeve as you walk past. Every vibration, badge, headline, and recommendation asks for a tiny decision: Should I look now? Should I answer? Should I save this for later? One decision is small, but fifty decisions before breakfast can leave the mind tired before real work even begins. The members of the morning club are not anti-technology. In fact, several of them work in technology. Their argument is not that phones are evil; it is that convenience becomes expensive when it quietly trains us to live in a permanent state of interruption.

心理學家常把注意力解釋成有限的資源。如果這是真的，那麼許多數位工具就像同一條狹窄街道上的商店，每一家都想在你走過時拉一下你的袖子。每一次震動、每一個通知角標、每一則標題、每一個推薦內容，都在要求你做一個小決定：我現在要看嗎？我要回嗎？我要留到之後嗎？單一決定很小，但如果在早餐前就做了五十個決定，真正的工作還沒開始，大腦就已經疲倦了。晨間俱樂部的成員並不反科技。事實上，其中幾位就在科技業工作。他們的主張不是手機很邪惡，而是當便利悄悄把我們訓練成永遠被打斷的狀態時，便利就會變得昂貴。

The most interesting change has not been productivity, although many members say they finish important tasks earlier than before. The deeper change is emotional. One woman realized that she had been waking up with a low level of fear every day, as if bad news might be waiting behind the screen. A young salesman said that he used to confuse stimulation with energy. If his brain felt busy, he assumed he was alive and engaged. Now he sees that busyness and clarity are not the same. Another member noticed that when she stopped beginning the morning with comparison, she felt less pressure to perform a perfect version of herself for strangers online.

最有趣的改變並不是生產力，雖然許多成員都說自己比以前更早完成重要任務。更深層的改變其實是情緒上的。有位女性發現，她過去每天起床時都帶著一種低程度的恐懼，好像壞消息正躲在螢幕後等她。一位年輕業務則說，他以前把刺激感誤認成活力。如果大腦感覺很忙，他就以為自己是清醒而投入的。現在他明白了，忙碌與清晰不是同一件事。另一位成員則注意到，當她不再用比較作為早晨的開端時，她就不再那麼有壓力，非得在網路上的陌生人面前表現出完美版本的自己。

This small experiment also reveals something about habit formation. People often wait for strong motivation before changing their behavior, but the club works because it asks for structure before motivation. The library opens at the same time. The box for phones sits in the same place. The table is always ready. In other words, the environment makes the choice easier. This matters because willpower is unreliable when we are sleepy, stressed, or lonely. Many self-improvement plans fail because they depend too heavily on heroic effort. The morning club succeeds because it respects human weakness. It assumes distraction will happen, so it designs a setting where distraction has fewer chances to win.

這個小實驗也揭示了習慣養成的一件事。人們常常等到自己很有動機時才想改變行為，但這個俱樂部之所以有效，是因為它要求先有結構，再等待動機。圖書館在固定時間開門。放手機的盒子擺在固定位置。桌子總是準備好的。換句話說，環境讓選擇變得更容易。這很重要，因為當我們想睡、壓力大、或感到孤單時，意志力並不可靠。許多自我成長計畫之所以失敗，是因為它們太依賴英雄式的努力。這個晨間俱樂部會成功，是因為它尊重人的脆弱。它假設分心一定會發生，所以設計出一個讓分心較難獲勝的場景。

Of course, the club is not a magical answer to modern life. Members still miss messages, doomscroll at night, and lose whole afternoons to unplanned browsing. Some stop coming after a month because the train schedule is inconvenient or because family duties change. Yet even those who leave often say the same thing: the club showed them that another rhythm was possible. Once you experience a morning that belongs to you, the usual digital rush feels less natural. You may still choose it, but you no longer confuse it with freedom. That realization is modest, but it is not small.

當然，這個俱樂部並不是現代生活的神奇解答。成員們還是會漏看訊息、在晚上無止盡滑手機，也會把整個下午浪費在沒有計畫的瀏覽上。有些人一個月後就不來了，可能是火車班次不方便，也可能是家庭責任改變了。但即使離開的人，常常也會說同一句話：這個俱樂部讓他們知道，原來還有另一種生活節奏是可能的。當你真正體驗過一個屬於自己的早晨之後，平常那種數位衝刺就不再那麼理所當然。你也許仍然會選擇它，但你不會再把它誤認為自由。這個體會很低調，卻一點也不小。

There is also a social side to the story. In large cities, people often search for community through loud events, expensive hobbies, or carefully branded lifestyles. The morning club offers something less glamorous but perhaps more durable: repeated presence. Nobody needs to be impressive there. Nobody needs to turn a private practice into content. By meeting again and again in a simple room, the members begin to recognize one another’s struggles without forcing confessions. They see who arrives tired, who keeps trying, who starts laughing more easily after a difficult week. Trust grows not from intensity, but from steady return.

這個故事也有社群的一面。在大城市裡，人們常常透過熱鬧活動、昂貴嗜好，或精心包裝的生活風格來尋找歸屬感。這個晨間俱樂部提供的東西比較不華麗，但也許更耐久：重複出現。不需要任何人在那裡表現得很厲害。也不需要把私人練習變成可以發佈的內容。藉由一次又一次在同一個簡單房間裡碰面，成員們開始理解彼此的掙扎，而不必強迫彼此告白。他們看見誰帶著疲憊來、誰持續努力、誰在艱難的一週後又比較容易笑了。信任不是從強烈時刻長出來的，而是從穩定回來的行動裡慢慢形成的。

Perhaps that is why the club has attracted attention beyond its size. Visitors sometimes expect a clever method for learning faster or working harder. What they find instead is a reminder that a good life may depend on small acts of protection. We protect sleep by going to bed on time. We protect health by eating with care. We may also need to protect attention by deciding, together if necessary, what does not get immediate access to our minds. In a culture that praises constant connection, that decision can look old-fashioned. But old-fashioned is not always outdated. Sometimes it is simply another name for wisdom that has become useful again.

也許這就是為什麼這個俱樂部明明不大，卻仍然吸引了外界注意。來訪者有時期待找到一種能更快學習或更努力工作的聰明方法。結果他們真正看到的，反而是一種提醒：好的生活，可能仰賴某些微小的保護行動。我們透過準時上床來保護睡眠，透過仔細飲食來保護健康；我們也可能需要透過先做決定來保護注意力，甚至在必要時一起決定，哪些事不該立刻進入我們的大腦。在一個讚美持續連線的文化裡，這個決定看起來也許很老派。但老派不一定代表過時。有時候，它只是另一個名字，指向那些再次變得有用的智慧。

If the members of the club have learned anything, it is this: attention is not only a mental skill but also a moral choice. What we attend to, day after day, slowly becomes the shape of our life. A person who gives every spare minute to noise should not be surprised when silence starts to feel uncomfortable. A person who keeps a small room in the morning for reading, thinking, writing, or simply noticing the world may discover a different truth. The mind does not become clear all at once. It clears the way a window clears after rain, little by little, until the city outside can finally be seen again.

如果這個俱樂部的成員學到了什麼，那就是：注意力不只是心理技巧，也是一種價值選擇。我們日復一日把注意力放在哪裡，那些東西就會慢慢長成我們人生的形狀。一個把每個零碎分鐘都交給噪音的人，不該驚訝於沉默竟開始讓自己不舒服。相反地，一個願意在早晨保留一個小空間給閱讀、思考、寫作，或只是留意世界的人，可能會發現另一個真相。頭腦不會突然變清楚。它變清楚的方式，更像雨後的窗戶一點一點變透明，直到窗外的城市終於又能被看見。

## 文法重點

### 1. present perfect continuous / ongoing result
- 規則：用現在完成式或現在完成進行概念表達「從過去持續到現在的影響」。  
- 中文說明：文章中雖然不一定每次都用完整進行式，但常用現在完成觀念表達改變持續到現在。  
- 文章例句：*her mornings were disappearing into short videos*  
- 新例句：*I have been spending too much time on my phone lately.*  
- 常見錯誤：不要把短暫一次動作誤寫成長期持續狀態。

### 2. not only ... but also ...
- 規則：用來強調兩個並列資訊。  
- 中文說明：這個句型很適合寫作時增加層次，不只講 A，也補充 B。  
- 文章例句：*attention is not only a mental skill but also a moral choice.*  
- 新例句：*The habit not only saves time but also reduces stress.*  
- 常見錯誤：前後結構要平行，例如都接名詞、都接形容詞或都接子句。

### 3. when + 子句, 主句
- 規則：用 `when` 引導時間或情境。  
- 中文說明：英文很常用 `when` 快速建立背景，讓句子更自然。  
- 文章例句：*when the streets are still quiet*  
- 新例句：*When my phone is in another room, I can read much longer.*  
- 常見錯誤：不要每句都只用 `because`，很多情境其實用 `when` 更自然。

### 4. may + 原形動詞
- 規則：`may` 可表示可能性。  
- 中文說明：比 `will` 更保留，也比 `maybe` 更正式。  
- 文章例句：*a good life may depend on small acts of protection*  
- 新例句：*This simple change may improve your focus.*  
- 常見錯誤：`may` 後面要接原形動詞，不要寫 `may improves`。

## 句型解析

### 1. *paper does not beg for attention every eight seconds.*
- 自然原因：把紙擬人化，語氣生動又好懂。
- 可替換部分：`paper` 可換成 `a book`, `a quiet room`, `a notebook`。
- 日常用法：你可以說 *My notebook does not beg for attention like my phone does.*

### 2. *convenience becomes expensive when it quietly trains us to live in a permanent state of interruption.*
- 自然原因：用 `becomes expensive` 表達不是花錢，而是付出心理代價，很有層次。
- 可替換部分：`convenience` 可換成 `speed`, `comfort`, `freedom`。
- 日常用法：可用來評論科技或工作方式，例如 *Speed becomes dangerous when accuracy disappears.*

### 3. *Trust grows not from intensity, but from steady return.*
- 自然原因：句子短、有節奏，而且對比清楚。
- 可替換部分：`Trust` 可換成 `Skill`, `Confidence`, `Friendship`。
- 日常用法：適合寫作結尾或口說總結，例如 *Confidence grows not from praise, but from repeated practice.*

## Collocations

1. **limited resource**：常用來談時間、金錢、注意力等有限資源。  
   Example: Attention is a limited resource in modern life.
2. **family emergency**：家庭緊急狀況。  
   Example: Members could use their phones only for a family emergency.
3. **mental clarity**：頭腦清晰。  
   Example: A quiet morning often brings mental clarity.
4. **heroic effort**：英雄式努力，指很拼但難以長久的做法。  
   Example: The plan failed because it depended on heroic effort.
5. **steady return**：穩定回來、持續出現。  
   Example: Trust grew through steady return, not dramatic speeches.

## 易混淆字

### 1. quiet / silent / calm
- **quiet**：安靜，通常指聲音不大。  
  Example: *The room was quiet at 6 a.m.*
- **silent**：完全沒聲音，強調無聲。  
  Example: *They sat in silent concentration.*
- **calm**：平靜，常指情緒或氣氛。  
  Example: *She felt calm after writing for twenty minutes.*

### 2. habit / routine / schedule
- **habit**：習慣，強調反覆形成的行為。  
  Example: *Checking messages early became a habit.*
- **routine**：固定流程，強調一套做法。  
  Example: *The club created a healthier morning routine.*
- **schedule**：時間表，強調時間安排。  
  Example: *His train schedule made it hard to join every day.*

3. Why does the article say convenience can become expensive?

### 答案

1. They wanted to take back their mornings from phones, short videos, and anxious checking.
2. They read, plan on paper, sketch ideas, copy poems, and sometimes talk briefly at the end.
3. Because constant digital interruption trains people to live in distraction and decision fatigue.

5. It grows through repeated presence and steady return, not dramatic moments.

4. 不需要任何人把私人練習變成可發佈的內容。

## 口說練習

1. **They come for something much simpler and, in modern life, much rarer: ninety minutes without their phones.**  
   朗讀提示：`much simpler and / much rarer` 要有對比感；在冒號前短停；重音放在 `simpler`, `rarer`, `ninety`, `phones`。
2. **Paper does not beg for attention every eight seconds.**  
   朗讀提示：`beg for attention` 要連讀自然；重音放在 `paper`, `beg`, `eight seconds`。
3. **Busyness and clarity are not the same.**  
   朗讀提示：這句要短而穩；重音在 `busyness`, `clarity`, `same`。
4. **The morning club succeeds because it respects human weakness.**  
   朗讀提示：`succeeds because it` 可順順帶過；重音放在 `morning club`, `respects`, `weakness`。
5. **Trust grows not from intensity, but from steady return.**  
   朗讀提示：`not from ... but from ...` 要讀出對比；在逗號處停一下；重音放在 `trust`, `intensity`, `steady return`。
